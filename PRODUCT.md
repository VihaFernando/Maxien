# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo media enthusiast managing their own anime, manga, and games in one place. Uses it daily at home  sometimes at the desk browsing new titles, sometimes on the couch in TV mode. They know what they want; they're not learning the interface, they're living in it.

## Product Purpose

Maxien / LifeSync is a personal entertainment hub: one place to discover, track, and play anime, manga, and games. Success looks like: open the app → find something → start watching in three taps or fewer. The interface should disappear into the content.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic. Restrained. Tactile.

The app should feel like a premium dark media surface: think Apple TV app meets Plex meets a well-crafted game launcher. Cover art is the star. Chrome is minimal. Every interaction has weight and feedback.

## Anti-references

- Streaming service bombast (Netflix/Crunchyroll heavy gradients everywhere, aggressive CTAs)
- MAL's utilitarian list aesthetic  this should feel curated, not spreadsheet-like
- Generic SaaS dashboard with cards, tables, metric chips  wrong register entirely
- Discord's saturated sidebar overload

## Design Principles

1. **Content is chrome.** Cover art, screenshots, and hero images carry the visual weight. UI elements recede; media advances.
2. **Every interaction has a response.** Hover, focus, press: always tactile. The interface should feel physical.
3. **Speed over ceremony.** TV mode especially: reach the episode in 2 key presses. No loading parades.
4. **Dark is native.** Dark mode is the primary state  light mode is the guest. Design dark first and lift for light.
5. **TV mode is a different surface.** 10-foot distance, d-pad input, large hit targets, huge cover art, Netflix-style rail browsing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast as baseline (4.5:1 body text)
- Full gamepad / d-pad navigation in TV mode
- Reduced motion support (`prefers-reduced-motion` + `maxien-reduce-motion` class)
- Large tap targets on mobile (≥44px)
